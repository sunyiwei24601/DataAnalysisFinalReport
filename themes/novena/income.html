Inequalities and Diabetes

Inequalities and Obesity

Obesity is believed to account for 80-85% of the risk of developing type 2 diabetes, while recent research suggests that obese people are up to 80 times more likely to develop type 2 diabetes than those with BMI of less than 22.

For obesity rate, income, education level, ethnicity, and age can be clearly discussed by group. Income level has a negative relationship with obesity rate. The obesity rate for adults with annual income higher than $75,000 is around 10% lower than the rate for adults with annual income lower than $34,999, which turns out that financial capability is a great attributor for the obesity rates.

We divided the data into 3 income level groups and conducted t-Test between groups. All the p-Value(5.89e-153) is much lower than 0.05 or 0.01 which proved our alternative hypothesis: There is an association between obesity rates and income.

Prevalence and Further Influence of Diabetes in Different Groups

Diabetes is increasing in the U.S., but varies widely by ethnic group. Non-Hispanic Black Adults has the highest prevalence of diabetes (14.248%) overall which is twice higher than White Adults. Males has higher prevalence of diabetes than females but not significant.

Even among patients with diagnosed diabetes, the impact of diabetes on their lives varies greatly. Diabetes is responsible for more than 60% of lower limb amputations not caused by accidental injuries[1]. American Indians has the highest amputation rate (8.879 %) overall, followed by non-Hispanic black adults (5.665%). Males has much higher rate of amputation than females. American Indians and Alaska natives has the highest mortality rate per 100,000 deaths, 129.8, followed by non-hispanic black adults with 102.23.

[1] source: Why Diabetes Can Lead to Amputation | Patient Advice | US News

People with diabetes are two to three times more likely to have depression according to CDC Report. Despite having the highest prevalence of diabetes, Black adult and hispanic adult patients have the lowest depression rate, 24.78% and 29.02 % respectively. Female patients have higher risk (35.72%) of developing depression compared with males (22.95%), which is possibly caused by body anxiety.
