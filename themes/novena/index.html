Start with Intro

Type2 Diabetes

Type 2 Diabetes is a common and chronic disorder in which blood sugar (glucose) levels are higher than usual. According to the National Diabetes Statistics Report[1], the US has more than 34.2 million people who have diabetes, which is 10.5% of the US population, and more than 95% of them have type 2 diabetes, which occurs when the body becomes resistant to the effect of insulin (the hormone that enables cells to take in sugar).

An estimated 88 million adults aged 18 years or older had prediabetes in 2018. Researchers have projected the number of US adults will continue to increase with 39.7 million in 2030, and eventually reach 60.6 million in 2060 [2].

Data Collection and Processing

U.S. Chronic Disease Indicators: Diabetes

This original dataset is provided by CDC with 124 indicators that were developed by consensus that allows states to uniformly define, collect and report chronic disease data

Nutrition, Physical Activity, Obesity Legislation

This dataset contains policy data for 50 US states and DC.Information related to state legislation on nutrition, physical activity, and obesity in settings.

Nutrition, Physical Activity, and Obesity

selected data from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey (ACS) on adult’s diet, physical activity, and weight status from Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System.

USDA Weekly Retail Food Sales

A weekly retail food sales series from 39 states by 10 product categories provided by the US Department of Agriculture.

Reddit Diabetes Posts and Comments

Posts and Comments under subreddit "Diabetes", including author, create time and content.

Fast Food Restaurants Across America

A list of over 10000 fast-food restaurants, including restaurant’s address, city, state, name, and website.
